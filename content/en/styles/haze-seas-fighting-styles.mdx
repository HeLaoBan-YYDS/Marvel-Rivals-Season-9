In the vast, treacherous waters of the Roblox world, your survival often depends on how well you can throw a punch. While many players focus solely on obtaining the rarest Devil Fruits, mastering **haze seas fighting styles** is often the secret to dominating both PvP and PvE encounters. Whether you are a newcomer starting in the First Sea or a veteran reaching the heights of the Third Sea, understanding the nuances of these **haze seas fighting styles** will give you a significant edge in battle.

Fighting styles represent one of the three primary damage-dealing systems in the game, alongside Swords and Fruits. Unlike Fruits, which can be lost or swapped, a fighting style is a permanent part of your toolkit that you can switch between at will once unlocked. This guide will walk you through the evolution of combat, from the humble beginnings of the Combat style to the devastating power of the Version 2 (V2) awakenings.

## Understanding the Basics of Haze Seas Fighting Styles

When you first spawn into the game, you are automatically equipped with the "Combat" style. It is the most basic form of martial arts, providing a simple M1 (left-click) combo and a couple of starter moves. However, as you progress through the First and Second Seas, you will quickly realize that basic combat is insufficient for the challenges ahead.

The game categorizes its martial arts primarily by their "Version." V1 styles are the base forms that you can purchase with Beli (money) from specific NPCs located on various islands. According to community reports, these styles are essential stepping stones. You cannot skip straight to the endgame powerhouses without first mastering the fundamentals.

### The Starter Trio: V1 Styles

In the current version of the game, players typically focus on three main V1 styles before looking toward the Third Sea for upgrades. These are Combat, Black Leg, and Electro. Each offers a unique flavor of combat, ranging from the fiery kicks of a chef to the high-voltage strikes of a mink.

| Fighting Style | Requirement | Cost (Beli) | Teacher NPC | Location |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Combat** | None | Free | Default | Starter Island |
| **Black Leg** | None | 50,000 | Blackleg Teacher | Sea Restaurant (First Sea) |
| **Electro** | None | 450,000 | Bunny Girl | Udon Prison (Second Sea) |

As shown in the table above, the cost increases significantly as you move toward the Second Sea. Electro is currently the most expensive V1 style, reflecting its higher damage ceiling and utility in mid-game farming.

## Detailed Breakdown of V1 Fighting Styles

Each style comes with a set of unique moves that consume stamina. Understanding the cooldowns and hitboxes of these moves is vital for effective "grinding" (farming NPCs for levels).

### Black Leg (V1)
Inspired by the legendary cook of the Straw Hat Pirates, Black Leg focuses entirely on powerful kicks. It is a favorite among beginners because of its relatively low cost and high mobility. Player experience suggests that Black Leg is particularly effective for bosses with large hitboxes, as its "Barrage" style moves can land multiple hits in quick succession.

### Electro Style (V1)
Electro is a step up in both price and power. It utilizes electricity to stun opponents, making it a viable choice for PvP. The Bunny Girl NPC in Udon Prison will teach you this style for 450,000 Beli. It features four distinct skills, providing a more versatile kit than the basic Combat style.

### Combat
While most players discard Combat as soon as they have 50,000 Beli, it remains a reliable fallback. If you ever want to return to your roots, you can talk to any fighting style teacher you have previously purchased a style from to re-equip Combat for free.

Community reports from the Third Sea indicate that the developers have been refining these styles to ensure they remain competitive with mythical Zoan fruits. ## Deep Dive into V2 Move Sets

To truly understand why players spend hours grinding for these upgrades, we must look at the specific movesets. The V2 versions aren't just "stronger"—they often change the fundamental way the style is played.

### Black Leg V2: The Azure Inferno
The most striking change in Black Leg V2 is the transition from orange flames to blue flames. In the world of [Roblox](https://www.roblox.com/) anime games, blue flames typically signify a much higher temperature and damage output.
*   **Demonic Crash:** A high-impact move that slams the target into the ground.
*   **Azure Inferno:** An upgraded version of the classic Diable Jambe, coating the user's legs in blue fire for massive burn damage.
*   **Stampede:** A teleportation-based move that allows you to close the gap between you and your opponent instantly.

### Electro V2: The Lightning Beast
Electro V2 focuses on "spammability" and speed. It is designed for players who want to overwhelm their opponents with a constant barrage of strikes.
*   **Electrifying Tackle:** A combo starter that can drag opponents into the air.
*   **Lightning Beast Barrage:** A move that summons a massive electrical beast to strike the target. According to player experience, this move is incredibly satisfying to land due to its wide area of effect (AoE).

### Cyborg V2: Mechanical Dominance
Cyborg V2 is unique because it feels like a blend of martial arts and heavy weaponry. It is one of the more "exaggerated" styles in terms of visual effects.
*   **Radical Beam:** A massive energy beam that resembles a Kamehameha. It causes significant screen shaking and deals heavy damage in a straight line.
*   **Radical Smash:** A powerful fist-based ground slam that creates a shockwave.

### Dragon Claw V2: The Draconic Might
Dragon Claw V2 is often cited by the community as one of the best styles for crowd control.
*   **Draconic Vortex:** This move acts as a vacuum, sucking in nearby opponents and stunning them. This makes it the perfect setup for a high-damage Fruit ability or a Sword skill.
*   **Draconic Flames:** A beam-like breath attack that covers a wide angle, making it difficult for opponents to dodge.

## Tips for Mastering Haze Seas Fighting Styles

To get the most out of your **haze seas fighting styles**, you need to think beyond just clicking the mouse. Here are some actionable tips from top-tier players:

1.  **Stamina Management:** Fighting styles consume stamina rapidly, especially the V2 versions. Ensure you are putting enough points into your Stamina stat to sustain a full combo.
2.  **M1 Chaining:** Don't just spam your skills. Use your M1 attacks to bridge the gap between skill cooldowns. Some styles have faster M1 animations than others, which can be used to "stun-lock" NPCs.
3.  **Elemental Intangibility:** Remember that standard fighting style attacks (without Haki) will not damage users with Elemental (Logia) fruits. Always ensure your Haki is active before engaging in PvP.
4.  **The "C3" Hub:** In the Third Sea, there is a specific area where many fighting style masters are lined up. This is a great place to test different styles and see which animations you prefer before committing to the grind.
5.  **Combo Starters:** Identify which of your moves has the lowest knockback. Usually, these are your best "starters." For example, the Electrifying Tackle in Electro V2 is an excellent way to launch an opponent before following up with a barrage.

The landscape of **haze seas fighting styles** is constantly evolving as the developers add more content. While Black Leg and Electro are the current kings of the First and Second Seas, the introduction of V2 awakenings has completely shifted the meta for endgame players. By choosing a style that complements your Devil Fruit and focusing on mastering the timing of your V2 moves, you can become a formidable force in any sea.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Which of the haze seas fighting styles is best for beginners?
For players just starting in the First Sea, **Black Leg** is widely considered the best choice. It is affordable (50,000 Beli) and provides excellent mobility through its jump and kick moves, making it much easier to navigate islands and dodge boss attacks compared to the basic Combat style.

### How do I upgrade my fighting style to V2?
To upgrade to V2, you generally need to reach the Third Sea and find the specific Master NPC for your style. You will likely need to meet certain requirements, such as a high mastery level in the V1 version and a significant amount of Beli or special items. Keep an eye on community updates, as the exact locations of these masters can change during major patches.

### Can I use fighting styles and swords at the same time?
You cannot use them simultaneously, but you can switch between them instantly using your hotbar. Many high-level players use **haze seas fighting styles** to stun an opponent (like using the Dragon Claw vacuum) and then quickly switch to a Sword to deliver a high-damage finishing blow.

### Does Black Leg V2 actually do more damage than V1?
Yes, Black Leg V2 (Azure Inferno) provides a significant damage boost over the V1 version. Not only are the base damage numbers higher, but the "Azure" status effect often deals more tick damage (burn) than the standard fire from the V1 style. Additionally, the moves have larger hitboxes, making them more effective in group combat.
