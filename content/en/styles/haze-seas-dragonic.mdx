## Unlocking the Haze Seas Dragonic V1 Style

To begin your journey with the haze seas dragonic style, you must first locate the Dragon Claw trainer. Community reports suggest that this trainer is usually found alongside the other style mentors in the main trainer hub. Unlike the mythical Dragon Fruit, which relies on low-percentage gacha rolls, the fighting style is a guaranteed purchase if you have the necessary currency.

The initial cost for the Dragonic V1 style is 50,000 in-game currency. Once purchased, you gain access to a set of moves that focus on raw physical power and fire manipulation. While the V1 version is potent for early-game leveling, it lacks the "flashiness" and extended hitbox range of its awakened counterpart.

## Evolution: Ascending to Haze Seas Dragonic V2

The real power of the haze seas dragonic style is unlocked through the Sage of Combat. This NPC is the gatekeeper for all fighting style awakenings. To evolve your Dragon Claw into its V2 form, you cannot simply pay money; you must prove your worth by gathering rare materials and gems.

According to recent gameplay showcases, the requirements for the Dragonic V2 evolution are among the most demanding in the game. You will need to farm specific bosses to obtain "Dragon Hearts," a rare drop that signifies your mastery over the draconic elements. Additionally, a significant amount of Gems—the premium or semi-premium currency of the game—is required to finalize the ritual.

## Mastering the Haze Seas Dragonic V2 Move Set

The V2 version of the haze seas dragonic style introduces moves that are designed for high-level synergy. The visual effects shift to a more intense, vibrant flame, and the "lag" or "delay" often associated with V1 moves is noticeably reduced.

### Dragonic Kick (V2)
This is an upgraded version of the Claw Kick. It travels further and hits with an explosive impact upon contact. In PVP, this is your primary tool for catching opponents who are trying to keep their distance. It can also be used as an escape tool if you find yourself surrounded.

### Dragonic Flames (V2)
This move allows the player to hold down the key to release a continuous, devastating stream of fire. Unlike the V1 version, the V2 flames have a wider spread and apply a "Burn" debuff that ticks for damage over several seconds. This is particularly effective against bosses with high health pools.

### Dragonic Vortex (V2)
The Vortex is the crown jewel of the haze seas dragonic arsenal. It creates a massive swirling funnel of fire and wind that sucks in all nearby enemies. Not only does it deal massive AOE damage, but it also leaves enemies stunned for a brief moment, allowing you to transition into a Dragonic Kick or a weapon skill.

## Strategic Tips for Dragonic Users

To truly excel with the haze seas dragonic style, you need to think beyond just clicking buttons. The synergy between your fighting style, your fruit, and your sword is what defines a top-tier player on the [Roblox](https://www.roblox.com) platform.

1.  **The "Vortex" Opener**: Always try to start your engagement with Dragonic Vortex. Because it pulls enemies in, it cancels their momentum and sets them up perfectly for a point-blank Dragonic Flames attack.
2.  **Burn Stacking**: The fire damage from Dragonic moves stacks with other burn effects. If you are using a fire-based fruit or a flaming sword, you can melt through enemy health bars by layering these DOT (Damage Over Time) effects.
3.  **Haki Integration**: Never forget to turn on your Buso Haki. While the base damage of Dragonic is high, Haki provides a multiplier that is essential for late-game bosses. Community reports suggest that Dragonic moves benefit significantly from the increased "hardness" and damage buff of high-level Haki.
4.  **Mobility Management**: Use Dragonic Kick not just for offense, but for positioning. The move has a slight invincibility window during the dash, which can be used to dodge telegraphed boss attacks.

## The Difference Between Dragonic Style and Dragon Fruit

It is a common point of confusion for new players: is the haze seas dragonic fighting style the same as the Dragon Fruit? The answer is no. While both share a draconic theme, they are separate power systems.

The **Dragon Fruit** is a Mythical Beast fruit with a very low roll chance (0.25%). It allows the user to transform into a literal dragon, fly, and use massive "Blast Breaths." However, while transformed, you generally cannot use your fighting style moves.

The **Dragonic Fighting Style**, on the other hand, is a martial art. It can be used in your human form and is often paired with other fruits (like String or Ice) to create versatile combat builds. Many players prefer the fighting style because it allows them to use swords and guns simultaneously, offering a more varied combat experience than the fruit's transformation.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Where can I find the haze seas dragonic trainer?
The Dragon Claw trainer is located in the main style hub, typically found in the First Sea. He stands alongside the trainers for Black Leg, Electro, Fish-Man, and Cyborg. You can purchase the base V1 style from him for 50,000 cash.

### How do I get Dragon Hearts for the V2 upgrade?
Dragon Hearts are rare drops obtained by defeating high-level bosses. Players often report the best luck farming dragon-themed bosses or participating in high-level sea beast raids. You will need two of these hearts to speak with the Sage of Combat for the V2 evolution.

### Can I use haze seas dragonic moves while using a Dragon Fruit transformation?
Generally, no. Most fruit transformations, including the Mythical Dragon transformation, restrict the use of fighting styles and swords while the transformation is active. To use your dragonic fighting style moves, you must revert to your human form.

### Is Dragonic better than Electro for PVP?
While "better" is subjective, many top-tier players prefer the haze seas dragonic style for PVP because of the Dragonic Vortex move. The ability to pull opponents into a stun is a massive advantage in fast-paced player battles, whereas Electro focuses more on stuns that can sometimes be dodged more easily.
