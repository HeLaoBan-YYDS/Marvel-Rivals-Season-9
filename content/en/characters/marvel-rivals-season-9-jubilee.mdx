## The Spark of the Strategist: Jubilee’s Ability Kit

Jubilee enters the roster as a Strategist, but unlike traditional healers who stay tucked away in the backline, she thrives on the edge of the action. Her kit revolves around her signature pyrotechnic plasma, which she can shape into both offensive and defensive tools. Based on early access player experience, her projectiles feel incredibly fluid, offering a "bouncy" rhythm that rewards players who can lead their shots and predict enemy movement.

Her primary fire consists of radiant energy projectiles that provide consistent chip damage to foes while offering minor restorative effects to allies caught in the splash. However, her true power lies in her secondary abilities. She can launch a seeking orb that travels through the environment, healing allies it passes and damaging enemies. The mechanical depth comes from her ability to "ignite" these orbs. When ignited, the orb releases a burst of energy that can apply blindness and vulnerability to enemies, making them easy targets for your Duelists.

## Mastering the Firework Ultimate

The ultimate ability, Fireworks Extravaganza, is perhaps the most visually stunning move in the game. When activated, Jubilee charges up a massive ring of outward-flying firework clusters. These clusters don't just look pretty; they launch nearby enemies into the air, disrupting their positioning and leaving them vulnerable to follow-up attacks from heroes like Iron Man or Scarlet Witch.

Community reports indicate that the ultimate has a unique "orbit" mechanic. After the initial blast, the clusters orbit Jubilee, creating a mobile zone of influence. Enemies inside this zone take continuous damage, while allies receive significant healing. The ability to change the radius of this circle during the ultimate's duration allows Jubilee players to adapt to the size of the objective, whether they are fighting in the tight corridors of Tokyo or the open spaces of the new convoy map.

## The Season 9 Team-Up Overhaul

Now, the system is more flexible, allowing players to choose their Team-Up loadout. Jubilee players can select between several powerful synergies, even if the required teammate isn't currently on the field—though having the teammate present grants an "Enhanced" version of the buff.

Even without The Hood present, Jubilee can utilize a specific Team-Up that boosts her damage output significantly. Player experience suggests that once the Team-Up is active, Jubilee can even land headshots with her fireworks, a feature usually reserved for Duelists.

## Thebes: The New Frontier

On July 23, the season will expand with the introduction of "Thebes," a new convoy map set in a high-tech Egyptian environment. This map is designed with Jubilee’s kit in mind, featuring numerous vertical platforms and tight chokepoints where her Fireworks Extravaganza can provide maximum value. The lore surrounding Thebes ties directly into the season's overarching narrative: The Mystery of Thebes.

In this storyline, Jubilee serves as one of Apocalypse’s Horsemen—specifically, the Horseman of Famine. Her motivation is deeply personal, as she hopes Apocalypse can help her son, Shogo, who has been trapped in his dragon form. This dark turn for the usually cheerful character is reflected in her "Horseman" skin, which features muted colors and vampiric energy effects.

## Strategies for Playing Jubilee

To excel with Jubilee, you must master the art of the "Ignite." Throwing your seeking orb is only half the battle; knowing exactly when to detonate it to catch the most enemies in the blindness radius is what separates a good Jubilee from a great one. Additionally, her shift ability, Radiant Leap, should be used offensively to gain a fire rate boost when your team is pushing, rather than just as an escape tool.

In the current meta, Jubilee pairs exceptionally well with tanks like Hulk and Magneto. Magneto's ability to group enemies together makes them prime targets for Jubilee’s vulnerability debuff. Meanwhile, the returning Gamma Team-Up allows Jubilee to provide a damage aura to Hulk, making him an unstoppable force on the front line.

| Strategy Tip | Why it Works |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Orb Management** | Don't always ignite immediately; let the orb travel through allies first to maximize healing. |
| **Shift Buff** | Use your Radiant Leap fire rate boost to help finish off low-HP enemies. |
| **Ult Positioning** | Activate your ultimate in the center of the objective to force enemies off the point. |
| **Team-Up Choice** | If you have a Blade on your team, always take the Night Walker Team-Up for the life-steal. |

### Is Jubilee a healer or a damage dealer in Marvel Rivals?

### How do I get the "Enhanced" Team-Up version for Jubilee?
To get the Enhanced version of a Team-Up, you must have the specific partner character on your team (e.g., Blade or The Hood). While you can use the basic version of the Team-Up with any team composition, the Enhanced version provides additional stats like life-steal or permanent fire rate boosts.

### What is the best Team-Up for Jubilee in Season 9?
Many players consider the "Night Walker" Team-Up with Blade to be the strongest for Jubilee, as it provides her with vampiric life-steal, significantly increasing her survivability during intense team fights.
